The Ethiopian Coffee Ceremony

Ethiopia, the birthplace of coffee, maintains a traditional coffee ceremony that is central to social and cultural life.

Ceremony Steps

The ritual involves roasting green coffee beans, grinding them with a mortar and pestle, and brewing in a jebena (clay pot).

Historic Landmarks

Ethiopia boasts numerous UNESCO World Heritage Sites dating back thousands of years.

Lalibela Rock-Hewn Churches

The 11 medieval monolithic churches carved out of rock in the 12th century.

Aksum Obelisks

Ancient stone monuments marking royal tombs in Ethiopia's first capital.

Traditional Ethiopian Cuisine

Ethiopian food is served on injera, a sourdough flatbread made from teff flour.

Popular Dishes

  • Doro Wat - spicy chicken stew
  • Kitfo - minced raw beef
  • Shiro - chickpea stew

Cultural Festivals

Ethiopia follows its own calendar and celebrates unique festivals.

Timket (Epiphany)

Three-day celebration of Jesus' baptism featuring colorful processions.

Meskel (Finding of the True Cross)

Festival marked by bonfires and dancing in September.

Ethiopian Calendar

Ethiopia uses its own calendar with 13 months. The current year is 2016 (as of 2023 Gregorian calendar).
